# Founding Thesis — *(working name: "Handoff")*

**Done-for-you Shopify store operations. Agency outcomes, software margins.**

Date: 29 June 2026 · Founders: Emmanuel + brother (design partner / domain lead)

---

## The one-sentence version

Shopify store owners don't want better *tools* — they want the work to *not be their problem.* We sell the finished outcome; AI does the labor in the background.

## The insight (why now, why this)

The tools to run a Shopify store are already cheap and good. The App Store is saturated with AI listing apps, and the cost of writing a product listing has collapsed from ~$30 of human labor to under $0.50.

And yet store owners **still pay humans $500+** to build stores and add products. We confirmed why with someone who's built dozens of these stores:

> "The apps are fine. People just don't want to bother."

That is the whole company. The bottleneck is no longer *capability* — AI solved that. The bottleneck is **effort and attention.** People will pay 1000x the software cost purely to not touch it. Everyone else is still shipping *tools* into a market that's drowning in tools. We ship the *outcome.*

## What we sell

The customer connects their store and says what they sell. They get back a fully populated, correct, ready-to-publish store/catalog. They approve. It goes live. They never operate a tool.

- **Front end:** "It's done." A human-feeling outcome, one approval click.
- **Back end:** AI agents chaining the work the existing apps and VAs do today — sourcing structure, listings, variants, images, pricing, SEO — assembled correctly, not generic AI slop.
- **Human-in-the-loop:** only for approval and the judgment calls AI still gets wrong.

## The wedge (and our unfair advantage)

Start with the single highest-frequency "don't want to bother" job: **getting a store fully populated with products, done right, hands-off.**

Our edge is distribution we already own: the brother has a live book of clients and a network of store builders/owners who *already pay for this.* We don't have a "how do we reach customers" problem on day one — and the Shopify App Store is a second built-in channel later.

## Business model

Outcome pricing, anchored against the $500 they already pay a human:

- Per store built / per N products added, and/or
- A monthly "keep it handled" managed plan.

Margins are software-grade because AI does the labor — that's the entire bet. Agency revenue shape, SaaS-style cost structure.

## The trap we're avoiding

"Done-for-you" can rot into a non-scalable agency. The escape is deliberate and sequenced:

1. **Concierge first** — Emmanuel + AI do the work by hand for the first customers, to learn the workflow cold and find every judgment call.
2. **Automate the back end** until a human barely touches it.
3. **Productize** into a self-serve outcome.

Do things that don't scale first; productize second.

## v1 — the next 2 weeks (we do NOT build the product yet)

The goal is to prove people pay for *done-for-you* over the cheap apps, with real money, before writing real software.

1. **Landing page** selling the outcome (not the tech): "Tell us what you sell. We hand you a finished store." One clear price.
2. **Brother pitches 10 real clients/leads** from his existing network.
3. **Take money** — pre-orders or deposits from the ones who say yes.
4. **Fulfill the first orders concierge-style** (Emmanuel + AI, by hand). Time it, log every manual step.

Success = real payment from people who could have used a $0.50 app and chose to pay us instead. That's the only validation that matters.

## What would make me (the YC partner) believe this

- 3+ paying customers in 2 weeks via the brother's network.
- They explicitly chose "done" over cheaper self-serve tools.
- The concierge job is automatable — most steps are AI-able, few need human judgment.

## Honest risks (tracked, not hidden)

- **Service vs. startup:** dies if AI can't do most of the labor. Mitigation: measure automatability during concierge.
- **Margins:** AI/API cost per store must stay well under price. Validate early.
- **Differentiation from agencies/VAs:** our moat is speed + price from automation + the trusted "done" brand, not just doing the same thing.
- **Platform risk:** built on Shopify. Acceptable for the wedge; broaden later.
- **Trust:** owners may not trust auto-publish to a live store. Mitigation: approval step, start with new/secondary stores.
